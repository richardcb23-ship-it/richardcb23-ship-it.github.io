About

Richard Calderón

Biomedical engineer from Lima, Perú. Currently pursuing my M.Sc. at UTEC, where I manage the Microfluidics & BioMEMS Laboratory (L503). My work sits at the intersection of engineering and biology — designing microfluidic devices, running CFD simulations, and fabricating functional prototypes.

Completed a 3-month research internship at the Lerner Research Institute, Cleveland Clinic (Biomedical Engineering), developing microfluidic devices for inertial CTC separation.

Co-founded DogMotion Studio, Perú's first 3D-printed personalized canine prosthetics startup — a project that went viral with millions of views across national and international media.

Microfluidics · Lab-on-a-Chip · Blood Processing

Lab-on-a-Chip: RBC Separation & Microencapsulation

Two integrated PDMS microfluidic devices. ErythroSep: inertial separation of RBCs, WBCs and platelets using serpentine CE channels. ErythroEncap: passive micromixer, flow-focusing droplet generator, on-chip CaCl₂ crosslinking. Validated with fluorescence microscopy and COMSOL CFD.

Inertial Particle Separation in Spiral Microchannels

Spiral microfluidic device for size-based inertial separation (2, 7, 10 µm). COMSOL Multiphysics: laminar flow + particle tracing, Dean flow focusing across CE regions. Parametric sweeps on Q and channel width. SLA 3D-printed device.
